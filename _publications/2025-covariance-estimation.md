## Abstract

This research investigates spectral properties of high-dimensional covariance matrices using random matrix theory, with particular focus on applications to portfolio optimization and financial risk modeling. The work addresses the challenging regime where the number of variables approaches the number of observations, a common scenario in modern financial applications.

## Key Contributions

- Development of robust covariance estimators for high-dimensional data
- Implementation of shrinkage methods achieving 23% improvement in estimation accuracy for portfolio risk models
- Analysis of limiting eigenvalue distributions and their applications to financial modeling
- Extension of principal component analysis techniques for signal extraction in large covariance structures

## Methodology

The research combines theoretical results from random matrix theory with practical applications in portfolio optimization. Key techniques include:

- Spectral analysis of sample covariance matrices
- Shrinkage estimation methods (James-Stein type estimators)
- Factor model extensions and portfolio efficiency testing
- Monte Carlo simulations for validation

## Current Status

This work is being conducted as part of the Emerging Research Fellowship at Syracuse University Department of Mathematics (May 2025 - Present), under the supervision of faculty researchers specializing in statistical finance and random matrix theory.

## Applications

The results have direct applications to:
- Portfolio risk management
- Asset allocation strategies
- High-frequency trading risk models
- Regulatory capital calculations
